Malay cuisine

Nasi Lemak

This dish is a Malay dish, originating from Malaysia, consisting of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk and served on pandan leaf with some sambal chilli paste. It is usually accompanied by a side of ikan bilis, a type of tiny fried fish or roasted peanuts. There are many different versions if this dish such as, Malaysian Chinese and Malaysian Indian versions, and Singaporean Malay and Singaporean Chinese version. Some consisting of fried or boiled egg with some sliced cucumber and chicken dumstick. Despite the many variations, many argue that without its iconic sambal chilli paste, the dish would not be complete.


Indian cuisine

Roti Prata

Roti Prata, also known as Roti canai or Roti chenai, is a fried flatbread that is cooked over a flat grilling pan. This dish orginated from Southern India. It is usually served with a vegetable or meat based curry and is also commonly cooked with cheese, onions, eggs and many other ingerdients.Some prefer pair it with sugar while other prefer curry. Due to the many prata stalls in Singapore, each having their own unqiue take on roti prata, the texture of the dough differs - ranging from soft and chewy to crispy prata, but most being somewhere in the middle of flaky and fluffy.


Chinese cuisine

Hainanese Chicken Rice

Hainanese Chicken Rice is a dish that adapted from early Chinese immigrants originally from Hainan province in southern China. Almost every country in Asia that has a history of immigrants from Chinahas a differnt version of this dish. In Singapore and Malaysia, this dish is known as "Hainan Chicken rice", but in Vietnam, it is known as "Hai Nam Chicken". Chicken rice is considered as one of Singapore's nation dishes. This dish is a common sight in Hawker centres and can be found in nearly all hawker centres across the country. It consists of poached chicken and rice, cooked with the broth of the chicken. It is usualy served with chilli sauce and slices of cucumber.
